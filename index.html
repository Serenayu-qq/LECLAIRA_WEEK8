Diners will be spoiled for choice at Cafe Liang's International buffet, which serves a wide variety of popular Asian and Western dishes. Healthy choices are also available from juice (during breakfast), fruit and organic salad counters. Outdoor dining is available during the spring/summer on the wooden deck overlooking the piazza and the former home of Chairman Mao. Those on the go should head to the Grab & Go counter for a quick bite. Enjoy cakes, sandwiches, salads and coffee.

Jasmine Lounge

The Jasmine Lounge has always been 'the place' to socialize while experiencing the East-meets-West breakfast, finest tea experience in a rich glamorous Art-Deco inspired furnishings. A specialty of the Jasmine Lounge is the first afternoon tea and Saturday Tea Dance, recreating the hotel founder Sir Victor Sassoon's favorite, back to the1930's.

Starbucks Reserve Roastery Shanghai

Opened in December 2017, the Starbucks Reserve Roastery in Shanghai is a flagship store located in the HKRI Taikoo Hui complex on West Nanjing Road. Spanning approximately 30,000 square feet over two levels, it was the largest Starbucks location globally at its inauguration

PARKS
Yuyuan Garden

Located in the heart of Shanghai's Old City, Yu Garden is a classical Chinese garden dating back to the Ming Dynasty. Covering approximately 2 hectares, it features traditional pavilions, ponds, rockeries, and intricate carvings, providing a tranquil escape from the city's hustle and bustle.

Fuxing Park

Situated in the former French Concession, Fuxing Park showcases formal French-style gardens with fountains, flowerbeds, and tree-lined paths. It's a popular spot for locals practicing tai chi, dancing, or playing cards, offering visitors a glimpse into daily Shanghai life.

Century Park

As Shanghai's largest park, Century Park combines British, Japanese, and Chinese landscaping styles across its expansive green spaces, lakes, and forests. Visitors can enjoy boating, cycling, bird-watching, or simply relaxing in its serene environment.

Zhongshan Park

Located in western Shanghai, Zhongshan Park is renowned for its beautiful gardens, serene lake, and the tallest statue of composer Chopin. The park's diverse plant life and peaceful ambiance make it a favorite among locals and tourists alike.

Jing'an Sculpture Park

Adjacent to the Shanghai Natural History Museum, Jing'an Sculpture Park is an artful oasis featuring modern sculptures amidst well-tended flower beds and water features. It's an orderly space perfect for contemplation and appreciating contemporary art in a natural setting.
